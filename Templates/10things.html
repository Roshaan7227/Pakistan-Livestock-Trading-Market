10 Things to Check Before Buying a Cow

Livestock Buying Guide

Ensure a Healthy and Profitable Purchase

Buying a cow is a significant investment. Whether for dairy, meat, or breeding, making an informed decision is crucial. Learn how to identify healthy cattle and what to look for before purchasing a cow to make a smart investment.

💡 Pro Tip

Always bring an experienced veterinarian or livestock expert when inspecting a potential purchase.

Physical Health Indicators

The first signs of a healthy cow are visible in its appearance and behavior.

1. Overall Health

  • Bright and clear eyes (no discharge)
  • Clean and moist nose
  • Normal breathing (no coughing or wheezing)
  • Shiny, smooth coat
  • Good appetite and alertness

2. Physical Appearance

  • Body condition score (not too thin or fat)
  • Strong legs and clean hooves
  • Healthy udder with no swelling
  • Active and responsive behavior

3. Health History

Ask for vaccination records and treatment history. Ensure the cow has been regularly vaccinated and dewormed. Request veterinary certificates if available.

"A healthy cow is a profitable cow. Always check the animal's health history and physical condition before making a purchase." — Expert Veterinarian

Breeding & Value Assessment

4. Breed and Lineage

Choose a breed suitable for your purpose - dairy (Holstein, Jersey) or meat (Angus, Hereford). Ask about the cow's lineage and parent performance.

5. Milk Production

Check daily milk yield and lactation stage if buying a dairy cow. Average dairy cows produce 20-30 liters per day depending on breed.

6. Age Verification

Verify age by checking teeth condition. Younger cows have 8 temporary incisors that wear down with age. Request birth records when possible.

7. Reproductive Status

Check pregnancy history and calving records. A healthy cow should calve every 12-14 months. Ask about any breeding difficulties.

8. Hooves & Legs

Ensure strong legs without swelling or cracks. Lameness is a major issue that affects productivity and requires expensive treatment.

9. Behavior & Temperament

Choose calm and manageable animals. Aggressive cows can be dangerous to handle and may affect milk production.

10. Market Price

Compare prices with market value before final purchase. Don't rush - visit multiple sellers to get the best value.

📋 Final Checklist Before Purchase

✓ Veterinary inspection completed
✓ Vaccination records verified
✓ Price negotiated and agreed
✓ Transportation arranged
✓ Quarantine area prepared
